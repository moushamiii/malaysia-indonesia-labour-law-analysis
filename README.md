#  Malaysia & Indonesia Labour Law Analysis

An interactive Tableau dashboard comparing labour law regulations in Malaysia and Indonesia, with a focus on working hours, overtime, maternity leave, and annual leave policies.

---

##  Project Overview

This project presents an interactive Tableau dashboard analysing labour law reforms in Malaysia and Indonesia between 2016 and 2025. Using publicly available labour legislation data, the dashboard compares key employment policies including legal working hours, overtime limits, maternity leave, public holidays, and annual leave entitlements.

Designed with a storytelling approach, the dashboard highlights how labour regulations have evolved over time and provides an accessible comparison of worker protections across the two countries.

---

##  Business Question

How have labour laws evolved in Malaysia and Indonesia, and what do these changes reveal about differences in worker protections and employment regulations?

---

##  Dashboard Features

- Comparison of legal weekly working hours
- Maternity leave reforms over time
- Legal working hours versus maximum potential working hours
- Comparison of leave benefits and public holidays
- Interactive country highlighting
- Narrative annotations explaining key policy changes

---

###  Tools Used

- Tableau

---
##  Key Insights

- Malaysia reduced its legal working week from 48 to 45 hours following legislative reforms.
- Malaysia significantly expanded maternity leave from 60 to 98 days in 2023.
- Indonesia maintained relatively stable working hour regulations throughout the analysis period.
- Although standard working hours differ by only a few hours each week, overtime regulations create substantially different maximum legal workloads.
- Differences in leave benefits highlight contrasting approaches to employee welfare between the two countries.
